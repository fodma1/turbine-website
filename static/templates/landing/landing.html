Combination therapies may be the best weapon against cancer, but designing them is costly and hit & miss

Due to tumor complexity, single drug therapies are often ineffective.

Rational combination therapies increase overall survival, improve trial success rates and provide clearer clinical benefits for payers.

But designing them is hard.

It takes years of basic research for a breakthrough combination. Even then, costly clinical trials are needed to validate the combination in vitro and in vivo - with abysmal success rates.

In silico simulations could speed up the process, but current solutions are more guesswork than science.

Medical science still hasn't understood how proteins interact with each other. This renders mathematical and genomic analysis inaccurate.

Turbine combines the latest omics research with AI to reliably simulate complex interventions.

Even with limited molecular knowledge, we are able to provide robust predictions using recent developments in complex system theory and years of our own research into the dynamics of biological signaling. Our mission is turning combination therapy design from a dark art into a streamlined, speedy process that’s part of every new drug trial.

Turbine finds combinations that work - in record time

Speed up combination therapy research

By screening combination candidates in silico, you can select the best drugs from a much larger set of candidates when designing clinical trials. As you don’t have to wait for in vitro experiments to run their course, all this happens in days instead of months.

Increase chance of approval for any compound

With several targeted therapies for certain cancer types, finding the right combination is extremely unlikely through trial and error clinical trials. But finding the right combination can shave months or years off trial time and decide a drug’s approval - authorities like the FDA fast track promising that show promising clinical benefits.

Increase patient survival significantly

Therapy response has been shown to increase up to 80% when patients are treated with combinations as opposed to singular therapies. Payers are inclined to cover longer treatments if progression free times increase significantly.

Extend drug lines

Not limited by a clinical trial’s necessary safety concerns or costs of in vitro experiments, Turbine can test combinations against any indication, discovering new therapeutic areas for drugs.

While still in development, Turbine has already made several discoveries that are consistent with the latest research.

Turbine is the only precise way to predict how cancer cells react to combination therapies

Effective combinations in silico

Based on a drug’s target and combination, Turbine combs through a compound library of existing therapy options. It identifies the combinations predicted to have the highest clinical benefit, way before clinical trials.

Customized dynamic biological network

Turbine uses the only large-scale biological network ready for dynamic analysis. Other solutions can’t do more than infer predictions from a static snapshot of human biology, like the genome. Turbine’s network was constructed based on the latest research into the proteome - the wiring diagram of the human body, customized using cutting edge omics data.

Powered by AI

Turbine's patent-pending artificial intelligence simulates perturbations in a network faster than any other algorithm on the market. To get statistically significant results, Turbine runs thousands of simulations per compound, lightning fast.

Additive toxicity before Phase I

Simulating the effect of combinations on healthy cell models as well as cancerous ones, Turbine calculates the combination's toxicity profile before any animal studies or Phase I trials.

Therapy resistant strain predictions

By introducing realistic simulated mutagenesis - thousands of random mutations - Turbine can pinpoint which strains would prove resistant or susceptible to therapy. As well as predicting likelihood of resistance, Turbine can serve as a basis of further research into how certain resistant strains can be suppressed.

Fight any cancer type

Turbine’s algorithms are network-agnostic, and can analyse any, arbitrarily large network if it contains data about the strength and direction of edges. Network biology researchers are encouraged to contact Turbine to test its capabilities on their own networks.
