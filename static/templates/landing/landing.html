Combination therapies may be the best weapon against cancer, but designing them is costly and hit & miss

Due to tumour heterogenity, single drug therapies are often ineffective. Rational combination therapies increase overall survival, improve trial success rates and provide clearer clinical benefits for payers.

But designing them is hard. It takes years of basic research for a breakthrough combination. Even then, costly clinical trials are needed to validate the combination in vitro and in vivo - with abysmal success rates.

Mathematical modeling has been used to screen combinations, but it cannot cope with the complexity of multi-drug interventions. Even cutting edge genomic analysis is useless, as combinations affect cancer cells in so many ways that their effects cannot be predicted based on static data.
